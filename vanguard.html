Vanguard Digital Advisor

Overview

Roles: UX/UI, Research, Prototype Development

Problem: We developed redesigns of Vanguard's Digital Advisor investing dashboard, in collaboration with Lead User Experience Researcher Mr. Steve Schang from Vanguard. The Digital Advisor is an automatic investing service that manages investments for long-term investments, making it ideal for new investors (students and young professionals), but this introduces many challenges in the development of a dashboard, such as differing levels of financial literacy, navigation, and more.

What I did: Over the course of three months, I utilized strategies of the UX lifecycle such as user interviews, personas, card-sorting, user flows and more to inform decisions I made when developing my UI kit and wireframes. I also conducted usability tests through UserTesting.com to receive feedback on my final design.

Reflection: From this experience, I implemented industry UX strategies, as well as developed my research and design skills. Balancing client needs and personal preferences also was a challenge:
  • Financial Dashboard: Developing an investing dashboard for individuals with limited financial knowledge meant that I had to conduct significant field research to evaluate which features mattered most. I also included features such as term definitions and pop-ups to help people understand financial concepts.
  • Staying on Schedule: Given our deadline, I had to make sure I was managing my research and development to remain on schedule.
  • Branding: Some decisions, like features of the dashboard, as well as the design (UI Kit), had to be inline with Vanguard's values and established branding. With limited contact to Vanguard, I also had to do individual research when making these decisions.

Initial Usability Test

Positive Findings Observed Problems
Recommendations

First, we evaluated the current prototype of Vanguard's Digital Advisor portfolio through usability tests.


Methodology: Our team observed college-aged and young-professional participants complete a list of tasks. Our participants had varying degrees of financial knowledge and experience. The usability tests helped us identify strengths and areas of improvement in the current site design.

These observations became the basis of our redesign.

Audience and Personas

Audience statistical data. Our team developed personas based on our user listening sessions.
Persona poster for a young professional. Empathy map evaluating our user's needs holistically.

We identified user needs based on user data and listening sessions.

Our Audience: Digital Advisor users are likely interested in investing, but may not have the experience or knowledge to do so. These individuals should be familiar with technology and have enough income to invest. Users are either students or young professionals from 18 to 30 years old age range.

User Data: A framework establishing basic demographics.

Listening Sessions: Our group conducted listening sessions to holistically understand our audience. We asked about their backgrounds, values, challenges, financial experience, what they wanted in an investing platform, and how they felt they could learn from their investing experiences.

Site Navigation and Structure

Kardsort.com for card sort testing. Card sort results, categorized.
Tree map indicating new site layout based on card sort data.

One major challenge experienced by our users: Navigation.


Methodology: We collected card sort data to determine more intuitive site navigation for new investors unfamiliar with typical investment portfolio navigation. Essentially, crucial Digital Advisor features were placed on cards then were sorted into pre-set or user-generated categories.

Results: We re-examined the site navigation and created a new sitemap based on user responses from our card sort. This lead to the creation of the "Help" page, as well as moving the "Goal Setting" feature to the main portfolio page.

Wireframing

Three different tasks and user flows. First draft wireframes.
Deliverable: Interactive Figma wireframes. Interaction chart for wireframes.

Highlighting user flow and seamless navigation, I developed wireframes with user feedback from our initial usability tests in mind.


Methodology: I created the initial sketches hand-drawn, then I developed wireframes in Figma. Interactivity I mapped simulated user flow and decisions, in addition to illustrating how viewers explored the site. The data from our usability tests, personas, site-map, and user-flow diagrams directed much of the wireframe development process.

Results: I delivered a fully-interactive Figma wireframe was completed. I emphasized the following elements:

  • Approachability: No overly-complicated jargon or layouts and a simple navigation bar.
  • Visualizations: Replacing the hurricane chart with a time series graph, pie-chart and bar graph for fund allocation, and tables for investment data.
  • Accessibility and Learning: Customer Support options and text explanations for financial services and terms.

UI Development

UI Kit

A UI Kit matching Vanguard's current branding was developed.


Methodology: Based on Vanguard's current brand and UI, I developed the UI kit: The navigation bar, UI colors, fonts, and visualizations were developed to match Vanguard's brand. Design notes:

  • Colors: Red based on Vanguard's current logo, however, green became the choice of color for buttons and charts as green is typically associated with financial growth and "go". All colors were pulled from existing Vanguard sites and materials. Color palette was limited to prevent distraction.
  • Font: Based on Vanguard's existing font. Conveys to users that Vanguard is established and reputable in the financial world, but that Vanguard is welcoming to investors at all financial levels.
  • Navigation bar: Includes redesigned site structure based on card sort data and user feedback. A help button was clearly labeled with a question mark icon.
  • Visualizations: Users reported preferring visualizations for reading data. A time series graph was used as the primary dashboard visualization as it is a straightforward representation of capital growth over a period of time.
  • Tables: Clearly summarize and display important financial details to the user.


Results: My UI Kit in Figma and documentation cataloging all design elements was developed.

Hi-Fidelity Mockup

Vanguard dashboard redesigned. Investment recommendations.
Helping options for users. FAQ for users.

Finally! A high-fidelity mockup of Vanguard's Digital Advisor Portfolio!


Methodology: All prior work, especially our wireframes and Vanguard's original prototype were referenced. I focused on the user-flows identified in my user-flowcharts: Locating and updating tax settings, receiving Digital Advisor customer support, and receiving investment recommendations. When developing my high fidelity mockup, I referenced my wireframes and the original Digital Advisor site. Interactive features include: a “regenerate recommendations” page and a “submit support ticket” flow. Visualizations were developed, the UI dashboard and navigation bar were refined to be less cluttered, and more financial explanation help was included.

Results: A deliverable fully-interactive Figma high-fidelity model was completed.

Mockup Usability Tests and Future Implementations

Usability Test Feedback and Recommendations Implemented Updates

Updating my high-fidelity wireframes according to usability test feedback.


"If this was around when I was younger… I could definitely have used something like this. I would recommend this to anybody who's starting to save for some goals and wants to invest." - User Tester #2


Methodology: We used UserTesting.com to facilitate usability tests for our high-fidelity mockups. Users completed a set of tasks and answered questions when evaluating our sites.

Results: With the user feedback we received, we were able to make adjustments.Overall, user feedback was largely positive. Users enjoyed the UI, dashboard, and visualizations, particularly the time series chart. Users also liked the explanations and recommendation features. Some updates and recommendations emerged:

  • Descriptions: Longer descriptions were added to more clearly explain specific services.
  • Usage: Some users were apprehensive to investing with a Digital Advisor. Possible future implementations could include explaining how the Digital Advisor makes investing choices.
  • Navigation: Some users had trouble locating certain features. Descriptions and buttons were added to direct users.
