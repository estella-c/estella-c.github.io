Search Trends

Interactive Gantt chart emphasizing patterns of "relevance" over the past 20 years. Interactive stacked bar graph to determine whether certain months have higher search volume.
Noted observations.

Referencing data collected by Google Trends, our team created several visualizations.


Project Question: How have search trends on subculture topics changed over the last twenty years?



  • Search Data: Over the past 20 years, Google Trends has tracked searches and categorized searches based on frequency and geographical location.
  • Relevancy: Specifically, we aimed to observe relevancy over time.

Process: Data over time necessitated graphs such as time series for overall trends. We created more interesting representations for relevance over 20 years with a Gantt chart highlighting relevancy, and determining search volume data categorized by month as a bar graph.


Walkability in NC

Project Scope. Variables identified in the dataset. Analysis.
Geographic interactive visualization (CBSA data). Interactive scatterplots comparing factors of correlation. Analysis.

I processed data from the EPA's Walkability Index dataset, and created visualizations for walkability in North Carolina.


Project Question: Which factors are the best predictors of Walkability Score?



  • Walkability: Measure of areas dedicated to pedestrians. Wikipedia defines “Walkability” as: “In urban planning, walkability is the accessibility of amenities by foot.” (Wikipedia)
  • The Walkability Index Dataset was collected in 2019 by the US EPA in cities across the country.
  • Variables: CBSA, employee population, average wage, etc.

Process: Given the data was geographical (CBSAs), and that I was trying to determine variables predicting walkability, I chose to represent my data as an interactive 1. geographic map and 2. a scatterplot with trendlines.


Observations:

  • Total population does not directly correlate with walkability.
  • Average employee population does.
  • However, high "low wage percentage" correlates to low walkability scores.
  • Areas of higher population density.
  • Walkability as an urban concept, rural areas have less formal public transit, walkable areas.
